§1 · Supported Standards

Every major publication format, mastered.

Each academic venue enforces its own distinct citation style, layout rules, structural requirements, and word limits. Our AI has internalized all of them.

IEEE
IEEE Conference & Journal
Two-column layout, numeric citations [1], structured headings in small caps. The gold standard for engineering and CS conferences.
Abstract ≤250 words 6-page limit Numeric refs Times New Roman 10pt
APA 7th
American Psychological Assoc.
Author-year citations (Smith, 2022), double-spaced throughout, running head on every page. Dominant in social sciences and psychology.
150–250 abstract DOI required Running head 1-inch margins
Springer
Springer LNCS
Single-column with strict 150-word abstract cap and 15-page maximum. Mandatory keywords section. LaTeX LNCS class required for final.
Abstract ≤150 (strict) ≤15 pages Keywords required
ACM
ACM Digital Library / SIGCHI
Two-column format with mandatory CCS Concepts classification system. ACM rights block in the first-column footer. ORCID verified authors.
150–200 abstract CCS Concepts Rights block
Scopus
Scopus Indexed Journals
Structured abstract with Background / Methods / Results / Conclusion headers. Mandatory DOI, ORCID, conflict of interest, and funding statements.
Structured abstract DOI mandatory ORCID
Elsevier
Elsevier Journals
Harvard-style author-year citations with a Highlights section (3–5 bullets, ≤125 chars each). Graphical abstract and data availability statement expected.
Highlights section Harvard citations Data statement
§2 · Methodology

How GenScholar works.

A layered pipeline of semantic parsing, format-specific rule application, and compliance verification — all driven by a large language model with deep academic publishing knowledge.

01
Paste your draft
Submit any research text — title, abstract, body sections, references — in any order or structure.
02
Select a format standard
Choose from IEEE, APA, Springer, ACM, Scopus, or Elsevier. Each triggers a distinct rule profile.
03
LLM semantic parsing
Claude identifies sections, citations, equations, tables, and figures — reconstructing the semantic structure of your paper.
04
Format transformation
Section headings, citation style, abstract structure, required sections (CCS Concepts, Highlights) are all applied automatically.
05
Compliance analysis
Every rule violation is flagged — errors that cause desk rejection, warnings to fix, and what's already correct.
06
Deep tools on demand
Run standalone abstract checks, citation validation, readability scoring, and AI title suggestions independently.
